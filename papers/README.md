# Papers

This folder contains paper summaries, reading notes, and literature maps.

## Purpose

The goal is to read papers not as isolated documents, but as components of a research map.

Each paper note should clarify:

- what problem the paper addresses;
- what assumption it makes;
- what method it uses;
- what evidence it provides;
- what it contributes to the broader research direction;
- what remains uncertain.

## Folder Structure

```text
reading-notes/     Detailed notes on individual papers
summaries/         Short summaries and synthesis notes
```

## Paper Reading Levels

**Level 1 — Orientation**

Understand the paper's problem, method, and main claim.

**Level 2 — Mechanism**

Understand how the method works and why it should work.

**Level 3 — Evidence**

Evaluate the experiments, metrics, baselines, and limitations.

**Level 4 — Integration**

Connect the paper to other papers, concepts, and research questions.

## Rule

Do not summarize papers only by abstract.

A useful paper note should include at least one of:

- mechanism diagram;
- limitation analysis;
- comparison to another paper;
- possible reproduction idea;
- research question generated by the paper.
