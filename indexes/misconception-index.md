# Misconception Index

Purpose: keep recurring conceptual traps visible during learning, paper reading, and public writing.

## Core Misconceptions

| Misconception | Safer framing | Check to apply |
| --- | --- | --- |
| descriptive similarity ≠ mechanism | Similar patterns can motivate hypotheses, but mechanism needs process-level evidence. | What operation, circuit, algorithm, or intervention supports the claim? |
| representation similarity ≠ same algorithm | Two systems can share geometry while computing it differently. | Is the comparison about outputs, latent structure, learning rule, or causal process? |
| brain-inspired ≠ biologically plausible | Inspiration can be loose design analogy rather than a claim about neural implementation. | Which biological constraints are actually modeled? |
| good prediction ≠ explanation | Predictive accuracy can be useful without identifying why the system works. | Does the model expose variables, mechanisms, or causal dependencies? |
| low-dimensional projection ≠ real neural manifold | A projection may reveal useful structure without proving the true latent geometry. | Is the manifold claim robust across methods, data, and perturbations? |
| normative model ≠ implemented mechanism | A model of what an ideal system should do is not automatically a model of how a brain does it. | Is there evidence for an implemented algorithm, not only an optimal objective? |

## Review Prompts

- Am I turning an analogy into a conclusion?
- Am I confusing measurement convenience with biological reality?
- Am I treating a benchmark score as mechanistic evidence?
- Am I ignoring behavior while comparing representations?
- Am I assuming a model learned the same solution because it solved the same task?
- Am I making a causal claim from correlational evidence?

## Use Cases

- Before finishing a concept note, check whether one misconception should be referenced.
- During paper reading, record which misconception the paper avoids or risks.
- During public writing, convert the relevant misconception into a clear caveat.
