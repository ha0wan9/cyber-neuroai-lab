# Roadmap

This roadmap organizes the development of Cyber NeuroAI Lab as a long-term learning and research system.

The goal is not to finish a fixed curriculum. The goal is to build a durable research scaffold that can evolve from course learning to experiments, literature synthesis, and original research questions.

## Phase 0 — Repository Setup

### Goal

Create a lightweight but durable structure for long-term learning and research.

## Phase 1 — Computational Neuroscience Foundations

### Goal

Build the mathematical, computational, and conceptual foundations needed for NeuroAI.

### Core Questions

- How can neural systems be modeled computationally?
- What is a neural code?
- How do neural systems represent information?
- How do neural dynamics evolve over time?
- How can we infer mental or perceptual variables from neural data?
- How do learning and plasticity reshape neural systems?

### Focus Areas

- Linear algebra for neural data
- Probability and statistics
- Dynamical systems
- Neural encoding models
- Neural decoding models
- Dimensionality reduction
- Bayesian inference
- Decision-making models
- Learning and plasticity
- Recurrent neural networks

### Expected Outputs

- Structured notes
- Small Python simulations
- Concept maps
- Weekly synthesis logs
- Flashcard candidates
- Research question backlog

---

## Phase 2 — NeuroAI Bridge

### Goal

Connect computational neuroscience foundations to modern AI models.

### Core Questions

- In what sense can artificial neural networks serve as models of the brain?
- How can we compare representations between brains and models?
- What does alignment mean between neural activity, behavior, and model representations?
- Can neural data guide AI model design?
- Can AI models help generate better hypotheses about cognition and neural computation?

### Focus Areas

- Representation similarity analysis
- Encoding models
- Decoding models
- Brain-score style evaluation
- Model-to-brain alignment
- Multimodal representation learning
- Brain-guided AI training
- Cognitive and behavioral modeling

### Expected Outputs

- Paper summaries
- Literature map
- Small probing experiments
- Research memos
- Initial hypotheses

## Operating Principle

This roadmap is allowed to evolve.

Each monthly review should ask:

1. What concepts have become stable?
2. What remains vague?
3. Which questions are becoming researchable?
4. Which notes should be turned into public outputs?
5. Which experiments should be implemented?
6. Which topics should be deprioritized?
