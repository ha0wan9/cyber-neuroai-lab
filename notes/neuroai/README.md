# NeuroAI Notes

This folder contains notes connecting neuroscience, cognitive science, and artificial intelligence.

## Purpose

The goal is to understand how biological and artificial neural systems can inform each other.

NeuroAI is treated here not as a single field with fixed boundaries, but as an interaction zone between:

- computational neuroscience;
- machine learning;
- cognitive science;
- representation learning;
- behavioral modeling;
- human-AI interaction.

## Core Questions

- In what sense can artificial neural networks model brain function?
- How can we compare representations across brains and models?
- Can neural data guide the design of AI systems?
- Can AI models help explain cognition and behavior?
- What are the limits of brain-AI analogy?
- What would it mean for AI systems to become better cognitive partners?

## Working Distinction

When comparing brains and AI models, separate:

- strict equivalence;
- formal analogy;
- functional similarity;
- engineering inspiration;
- metaphor.

This distinction is important to avoid overclaiming.
